## 1. The brief in one paragraph

Da Vinci Consulting Services helps behavioral health organizations in Arizona (and beyond) win at compliance, Medicaid credentialing, Joint Commission accreditation, state licensing, and operational leadership. The audience is **founders, executives, and clinical/compliance directors of treatment centers and clinics** — people who are stretched thin and afraid of a failed audit. The site's single job: **make a stressed operator feel that one expert team can take the regulatory weight off their shoulders, then book the consultation.** The proof is real: 13+ years, 50+ facilities, 98% retention, $10M+ in credentialed Medicaid contracts, founder Tony Renello (MS, LISAC, VP of ABCAC).

## 2. Brand thesis — "Renaissance precision for behavioral healthcare"

The existing brand already gives us the strongest possible hook and then squanders it: **the Vitruvian Man** and the Da Vinci notebook aesthetic. Leonardo is the patron saint of *proportion, mastery, and the union of art and science.* That is exactly what good compliance consulting is — bringing order and proportion to regulatory chaos so the human at the center (the patient, the clinician) is in balance.

So the whole design is built on one idea: **proportion creates confidence.** Where PCW is the energetic phoenix (navy + orange, motion, modern), Da Vinci is the master's studio — gold, ink, parchment, measured geometry, quiet authority. Same engineering caliber, opposite temperament. This keeps the two sister sites clearly related (both Phoenix Creative Works builds) but never twins.

**What we keep from the current brand:** the antique gold accent, the Da Vinci / Vitruvian motif, the warm parchment tone, the wordmark.
**What we elevate:** refine the muddy olive-gold into a richer antique gold; trade the muddy sepia photo-hero for a disciplined proportion-driven hero; add a real type system; add structure (mega menu, animated process, stat band, rich footer); and turn the Vitruvian idea into a *system*, not a single background image.

## 3. The signature — the Vitruvian Proportion System

This is the one thing the site is remembered by. Spend the boldness here; keep everything else quiet.

A recurring **circle-inscribed-in-square** geometric motif and **hairline proportion lines / golden-ratio framing**, drawn like ink on a notebook page. It shows up as:

- A faint, large, off-canvas **circle-in-square line drawing** behind the hero (very low opacity gold/ink hairlines, not the literal Vitruvian photo).
- **Corner ticks and proportion marks** on cards and image frames (small L-shaped registration marks at corners, like a draftsman's crop marks).
- The **process / phases timeline** connected by a hairline arc (an echo of the Vitruvian circle) rather than a straight line — this is also where the animation reference from the recovery site comes in (§8).
- A **"compliance in balance" diagram** on the home page: two overlapping rings (Regulation ∩ Care) labeled like a Renaissance schematic — the same overlapping-circles device the reference site used for Mental Health / Substance Use, re-skinned in ink + gold.
- Section eyebrows set like **notebook annotations** (mono, uppercase, with a short rule), as if hand-noted in a margin.

Rule: the geometry is always **hairline, low-contrast, and structural** — it frames content, it never shouts. One gold line is worth ten.

- Secondary CTA = transparent with `--ink` 1px border on light / `--cream` border on dark; hover fills `--ink`/`--gold` faintly.
- Dark sections use `--ink` ground, `--cream` headline, `--cream-muted` body, `--gold` accents — this is the "stat band," the CTA band, and the footer.
- **Don't** put small gold text on parchment (fails contrast). Use `--gold-deep` for small gold text, `--gold` only for large display or on dark.
- Forest is a supporting voice, not a co-lead — roughly a 70 / 22 / 8 split of ink-neutral / gold / forest.

## 5. Typography

Pair a characterful **old-style serif display** (the Renaissance hook) with a **clean neutral grotesque** (keeps it modern and legible), plus a **mono used only for annotations** (the notebook-margin device). All free / Google Fonts so Claude Code can self-host or link easily.

| Role | Typeface | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Display / headlines | **Fraunces** (variable; opsz on, SOFT axis up, slight WONK) | Hero, section H2s, big numbers. Use weight 500–600, optical size large. This face carries the "made by a master, slightly antique" personality. Tight leading (1.0–1.05), small negative tracking on large sizes. |
| Body / UI | **Inter** | All paragraph text, nav, buttons, labels, card titles. Neutral, trustworthy, dense legibility. |
| Annotation / data | **IBM Plex Mono** (or Geist Mono) | ONLY for eyebrows, stat labels, "registration mark" captions, breadcrumb. Uppercase, tracked +0.12em, small. Evokes a draftsman's margin notes. Use sparingly. |

## 6. Spacing, grid, radius, elevation

- **Grid:** 12-col, max content width 1200px, gutter 24px, page padding 24px (mobile) → 80px (desktop).
- **Vertical rhythm:** section padding 96px → 128px desktop, 56px → 72px mobile. Be generous; this brand breathes.
- **Spacing scale (px):** 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128.
- **Radius:** cards `14px`, buttons `10px` (pill optional for the primary CTA to echo PCW — pick one and stay consistent; recommend **pill for primary CTA, 10px for cards/inputs** to mirror PCW's pill buttons while keeping a more "architectural" card corner). Image frames `16px`.
- **Borders:** 1px `--line` is the default separator. The proportion-mark corner ticks (§3) are decorative `--gold` at 1px.
- **Elevation:** keep shadows soft and warm, never blue. Card rest: `0 1px 2px rgba(33,28,24,.04)`; card hover: `0 12px 32px rgba(33,28,24,.10)` + 1px `--gold` border fade-in. Dark-section panels use border + inner glow, not drop shadow.

- **Icons:** a single thin, geometric line set (Lucide is fine, 1.5px stroke) tinted `--gold` or `--forest`, never filled-color. Optionally house key service icons inside a small `--cream` rounded square with a 1px `--line` border (like PCW's icon chips) for the mega menu and cards.

Apply a subtle warm/parchment duotone or a gentle gold-ink gradient overlay so photos sit inside the palette (the current site's people-photo in the mobile menu is the right energy; the sepia Vitruvian photo is *not* — retire it as a literal background).

- **Partner / trust logos:** Medicaid, The Joint Commission, AHCCCS, Arizona Dept. of Health Services, ABCAC. Render mono/desaturated in a quiet "Trusted by / Measured by the same standards you are" strip.

## 8. Motion system

Motion should feel like ink being drawn and proportion being measured — deliberate, never bouncy. Use Framer Motion (or CSS) with an `ease-out` / custom cubic-bezier `(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)`. **Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`** (disable transforms, keep opacity).

Folding in the animation references you liked (the phased treatment timeline with dashed connectors + the overlapping circles):

1. **Scroll-reveal:** sections fade + rise 16–24px, staggered 60–80ms across children. Subtle.
2. **Hero proportion draw-in:** on load, the circle-in-square hairline geometry *draws itself* (SVG stroke-dashoffset animation, ~1.2s) behind the headline, then settles to low opacity. One orchestrated moment, then stillness.
3. **Process / phases timeline (§9, §13.1):** the connecting **hairline arc draws left-to-right** as you scroll into it, and each phase card pops its icon + lifts in sequence — directly inspired by your reference site's Phase One→Five timeline, re-skinned with the gold arc instead of a dashed line.
4. **"Compliance in balance" diagram:** the two overlapping rings (Regulation ∩ Care) gently scale/breathe into overlap on scroll, the intersection filling with a soft gold — the reskinned Mental-Health ∩ Substance-Use device from your reference.
5. **Stat counters:** numbers count up (50+, 98%, 15+, $10M+) once on enter.
6. **Card hover:** lift 4px, `--gold` border fades in, corner registration ticks brighten. 150ms.
7. **Mega menu:** fade + 8px drop-in, 180ms; feature card image has a slow ambient scale (1.0→1.04 over 8s) on open.
8. **Buttons:** label and arrow nudge 2px on hover; gold→gold-bright fill transition 150ms.

Restraint: pick these moments and stop. No parallax-everything, no constant floaty loops except the one ambient hero geometry.
